📚 Learn Chess

♟ Basics

Chess is played on an 8x8 board. Each player has 16 pieces and the goal is to checkmate the opponent’s king.

  • Pawns, rooks, knights, bishops, a queen, and a king make up each side
  • Each piece moves differently
  • The goal is to trap the opponent's king (checkmate)

♟ Openings

Openings are the first moves of the game. The goal is to control the center and develop pieces effectively.

  • Control the center with pawns (e4, d4)
  • Develop knights and bishops early
  • Castle your king for safety

⚔️ Middlegame

The middlegame is where most battles occur. Players focus on tactics, positioning, and attacking strategies.

  • Look for tactics such as forks, pins, and skewers
  • Control important central squares
  • Coordinate your pieces effectively
  • Create attacking chances against the opponent’s king

Strong planning and awareness can give you the advantage in this phase.

🏁 Endgame

The endgame occurs when few pieces remain. Precision and technique are critical in this phase.

  • Activate your king and bring it into play
  • Promote pawns to gain advantage
  • Learn key checkmate patterns
  • Control key squares and limit the opponent’s king

Good endgame skills turn small advantages into victories.
